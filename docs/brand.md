# Probell — Brand & Experience
**Last updated: 28 April 2026**

---

## Purpose

Probell is a modern supplement brand focused on strength, performance, and consistency.

The website is a **conversion-focused experience** that presents products clearly, builds trust, and makes decisions easy.

It is not a marketplace, a content blog, or an animation showcase.
It is a **focused ecommerce experience**.

---

## Brand Principles

### 1. Clarity over noise
Users must understand what Probell is, what it sells, and why it matters within seconds.

Avoid unnecessary content, vague messaging, and overly complex layouts.

### 2. Product-first
The product is the center of every experience. Visuals highlight the product. Sections support product understanding. Nothing distracts from the core offering.

### 3. Confidence over hype
The brand tone is disciplined, reliable, and performance-driven.

Avoid exaggerated marketing, aggressive fitness clichés, and influencer-style branding.

### 4. Bold and simple
High impact does not mean high complexity. Large type, real photography, minimal chrome. The client reference direction: gritty gym aesthetic, strong copy, product as hero.

### 5. Built for growth
The system must support new products, future features, and CMS integration. Avoid hardcoded assumptions and tightly coupled UI.

## Brand Voice

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| "This is not a kettlebell. It's protein." | "Unleash your potential" |
| "The only supplement you can lift." | "Premium quality formula" |
| "Built for the grind." | "Take your fitness to the next level" |

Short. Provocative. Confident. No fluff.

## Experience Principles

### 1. Guided progression
Each section builds on the previous one. The user should feel led through a journey, not dropped into a layout.

**Homepage flow:**
1. Introduction — brand + product hook
2. Reveal — product takes center stage
3. Exploration — flavors, variants, options
4. Reinforcement — benefits, lifestyle, trust signals
5. Action — clear CTA

### 2. Interactions support content
Interactions must serve understanding, not decorate it.

- Subtle and purposeful
- No animation for animation's sake
- No unpredictable behavior

The scroll-driven hero is the primary interaction pattern. It is justified because the user physically "lifts" the product into view — the mechanic reinforces the tagline.

### 3. Controlled visual rhythm
Alternate between visual intensity and breathing room. Use the dark background as negative space, not dead space.

### 4. Consistency in structure
Sections follow predictable patterns. The user should never need to re-learn the interface.

---

## Success Criteria

- A user understands the product offering in under 3 seconds
- A user can navigate to a product without confusion
- The brand feels trustworthy and structured
- The UI feels intentional, not template-based

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Disconnected sections with no flow
- Multiple competing visual elements at equal weight
- SaaS/dashboard-style layouts
- Treating the page as a generic template
- Prioritising effects over clarity
- Exaggerated fitness marketing copy
